Enrique Alfonso was born in Habana, Cuba, 1989. He is, a painter, draftsman, craftsman, and graphic humorist. His fascination for art comes from an early age. Influenced greatly in part, by his paternal grandmother. An avid enthusiast of plastic arts, and in her own right, an artist herself. In addition to his native language, Spanish, he studied, English and French, in the hopes to liaise, with other cultures. At the age of 16 he began working in a ceramic workshop, owned by a family friend. Three years later, he started his own studio. This allowed him to commit all of his time, to his passion, and delve fully into the world of arts. In 2018, he met Valera, or as he likes to call him "maestro". Also a Cuban plastic artist, who soon became his instructor, colleague and friend. It was he, who inspired him to create his ópera prima, “Luz de luna " (moonlight).

Each of Enrique's paintings, strive to tell a story. His contemporary art pieces, are full of symbolisms, taking you to a place, where Magical Realism, meets; Pop Surrealism, and Expressionism. Elements, and characters that derive, from the artist memories, transport you, into Enrique’s own world of imagination. And by using familiar objects which many cubans have known or lived with, for generations, he allows for his paintings to tell the tale themselves. In the artist pursuit, to create a connection with the spectator, he conveys to capture human feelings on the canvas, a task he accomplishes with true, skilled, artistry.

Creating a collector’s piece, in each one of them!

External credits, include, several personal exhibitions, and has also participated in numerous collective exhibitions, inside and outside of Cuba.

He has won several awards in national, as well as, international competitions!

(See accolades below)

He currently lives and works in Havana, Cuba; in hopes of reaching other horizons!
